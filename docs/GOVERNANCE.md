## Executive Summary

Otic Fortress governance defines how AI systems are supervised, approved, explained, measured, and aligned to enterprise policy. The governance model connects AI Shadow observations, Policy Engine decisions, Risk Engine scoring, compliance evidence, trust scores, and human approval workflows.

## Purpose

This document is the master index for AI governance documentation in Otic Fortress. It establishes the governance vision and links the supporting framework documents.

## Scope

Governance applies to AI agents, model integrations, prompts, tool calls, data access, automated decisions, approval workflows, compliance mappings, dashboards, and audit evidence generated by Otic Fortress.

## Audience

AI engineers, governance leads, risk teams, compliance officers, security engineers, product owners, enterprise architects, auditors, and customer-facing implementation teams.

## Business Context

Enterprises need AI systems that can act quickly without becoming unaccountable. Otic Fortress governance provides a framework for safe automation by defining who may approve actions, how policy is enforced, how risk is scored, and how decisions are explained.

## AI Governance Vision

The governance vision is to make every AI action policy-aware, risk-aware, explainable, and attributable to an accountable owner.

## Governance Principles

| Principle | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| Accountability | Every AI action has an owner, actor, and audit trail. |
| Explainability | Decisions must expose the policy and risk rationale. |
| Proportional Control | Higher-risk actions receive stronger approval and monitoring. |
| Human Oversight | Humans approve actions that can cause material impact. |
| Continuous Governance | Governance is evaluated before, during, and after AI activity. |

## AI Shadow Governance

AI Shadow observes prompts, tool calls, context usage, model outputs, decisions, approvals, and resulting system actions. It provides the evidence layer for governance and compliance.

## Policy Engine Overview

The Policy Engine evaluates whether an AI action is allowed, denied, transformed, escalated, or routed for approval. See [Policy Lifecycle](governance/policy-lifecycle.md) and [Policy Engine](security/policy-engine.md).

## Risk Engine Overview

The Risk Engine scores action risk using identity, data sensitivity, tool capability, business impact, historical behavior, and environment context. See [Risk Engine](security/risk-engine.md).

## Trust Score Overview

Trust Score summarizes confidence in an AI actor, workflow, or action based on risk signals, historical compliance, policy outcomes, and monitoring evidence. See [Trust Score](governance/trust-score.md).

## Explainability Overview

Explainability provides readable rationale for policy decisions, risk scores, approval requirements, and compliance outcomes. See [Explainability](governance/explainability.md).

## Human Approval Model

Human approval gates are used for high-risk, regulated, irreversible, or customer-impacting AI actions. See [Human Approval](governance/human-approval.md).

## Compliance Mapping

Governance controls are mapped to compliance obligations, control owners, evidence sources, and review cadence. See [Compliance Mapping](governance/compliance-mapping.md).

## Governance Dashboards

Dashboards show AI activity, risk distribution, approval queues, policy outcomes, exceptions, violations, trust trends, and compliance evidence completeness.

## Governance Ownership

The Cloud, Security & Governance Lead owns the AI governance model, policy architecture, risk controls, compliance alignment, and approval governance strategy for Otic Fortress.

## Core Components

| Component | Governance Role |
|-----------|-----------------|
| AI Shadow | Captures observed AI behavior and evidence. |
| Policy Engine | Applies governance rules. |
| Risk Engine | Scores action and actor risk. |
| Approval Gateway | Routes decisions to accountable humans. |
| Compliance Engine | Maps evidence to obligations. |

## Data Flow

AI activity is captured by AI Shadow, evaluated by policy, scored by risk, optionally routed for approval, executed only when authorized, and recorded as governance evidence.

## Azure Services Used

Microsoft Entra ID, Azure Monitor, Log Analytics, Microsoft Sentinel, Azure Storage, Azure Key Vault, Azure API Management, and Azure Policy support governance telemetry and enforcement.

## Security Considerations

Governance workflows must protect sensitive prompts, model responses, approvals, evidence records, and compliance mappings from unauthorized access or tampering.

## Governance Considerations

Policies must be versioned, reviewed, explainable, tested, and traceable to business requirements.

## Monitoring and Observability

Governance observability tracks policy decisions, risk scores, approval latency, exception volume, trust score trends, and compliance control health.

## Failure Scenarios

| Scenario | Expected Control |
|----------|------------------|
| Approval queue unavailable | Hold high-risk actions until approval service recovers. |
| Policy version conflict | Use the last approved policy version and create an exception record. |
| Evidence ingestion failure | Preserve local evidence and alert governance owners. |

## Configuration Guidance

Configure policy repositories, approval groups, trust score thresholds, compliance mappings, data retention rules, and dashboard access by environment.

## Design Decisions

Governance decisions are documented in [ADR-007-AI-Governance](adr/ADR-007-AI-Governance.md), [ADR-009-Human-Approval](adr/ADR-009-Human-Approval.md), [ADR-010-Policy-Engine](adr/ADR-010-Policy-Engine.md), and [ADR-011-Risk-Engine](adr/ADR-011-Risk-Engine.md).

## Trade-offs

Governance may slow high-risk automation, but it improves auditability, customer trust, regulatory readiness, and operational accountability.

## Future Enhancements

Future work includes automated policy simulation, governance evidence exports, trust score explainability improvements, and adaptive approval thresholds.
